WHY AMOSKYS EXISTS

A patient on the other end of an alert

I spent a year on a SOC desk defending 1,200 networked medical devices at Hunterdon Healthcare. Infusion pumps, imaging modalities, bedside monitors. Boxes the vendor would not let me patch and the clinical team could not afford to unplug. One shift I sat with the thought that if I missed an alert, a patient would be on the other end of it, and I could not stop thinking about it.

That is the moment AMOSKYS started, even though I did not have a name for it yet. I wanted every place that could be attacked to be watched by something that would tell the truth about what happened, in a way that no attacker could rewrite, so that the people depending on those machines would never be quietly harmed in the dark.

I started with what I had. A Mac. A hard disk. One peripheral agent. Then another. Then a correlator. Then a detection brain. Then a dashboard. Today AMOSKYS is a signed, notarized macOS endpoint security product running the same philosophy I began with.

In March 2026 Apple granted the Endpoint Security Framework development entitlement to AMOSKYS in nine days, against a typical wait of months. The system extension is in design now, and the distribution entitlement application is the next milestone. The dense agent mesh that exists today gives AMOSKYS its eyes; ESF will give it kernel-level reflexes.

THE PHILOSOPHY

Five layers, integrity as a primitive

Any place that can be attacked has to go through something. A kernel syscall, a network socket, a disk write, a DNS lookup, a credential use. Every surface is a chance to see, and every seen event is a chance to prove. AMOSKYS is built on five layers, with integrity as a first-class primitive.

  1. Dense peripheral coverage Every attack surface gets a single-purpose probe mapped directly to a MITRE ATT&CK technique. Not one monolithic sensor, a mesh of 197 probes across 13 agents, the way a nervous system covers a body.
  2. Cryptographic log integrity Every log entry is signed at the moment of creation. Ed25519 on the wire, append-only SQLite WAL on disk. The first assumption is that an attacker will try to lie to the logs. The design makes the lie mathematically detectable.
  3. Context and correlation An AgentBus blackboard clusters events by host, user, process tree, and time window. DNS and geo enrichment agents add external context. Deduplication keeps the detection layer clean so the humans downstream only see things that matter.
  4. Heterogeneous detection brains Different anomalies need different models. XGBoost for volumetric signals. IsolationForest for behavioral outliers. LSTM autoencoders for sequences. MLP for classification. Each detector gets the brain that matches the physics of its signal.
  5. Adaptive retraining (AMRDR) A closed feedback loop where analyst dispositions and outcomes refine model weights. The system gets better at the environment it lives in instead of decaying into alert fatigue.

Sensors. Signed logs. Correlation. Heterogeneous brains. Adaptive learning. Five layers, stacked.

VISION

Defending what cannot be patched

AMOSKYS is a macOS endpoint security product today. That is the first substrate. The second is industrial and manufacturing OT, passive only, process-aware, decoding Modbus, DNP3, EtherNet/IP, Siemens S7, and OPC UA, running the same five-layer architecture on a different wire and a different physics. The third substrate is whatever cannot be patched next.

The thesis underneath all of it is simple. The things that cannot be patched are the things that carry the most risk, and the people who depend on those things deserve a defender that tells the truth. AMOSKYS is my attempt to build that defender in the open, one substrate at a time, with integrity as a primitive and detection content anyone can read.

HOW IT'S BUILT

Honesty about the tools I use

AMOSKYS is designed, scoped, validated, and shipped by me. I use modern AI coding assistants as an accelerator for implementation, the same way an engineer in 2010 used Stack Overflow and an engineer in 2000 used O'Reilly books. Every detection philosophy, every probe-to-MITRE mapping, every architectural decision, every priority call is mine. The product knows what it is because I do.

I believe being transparent about this is the only honest way to build security tools in the era we are living through. The defenders who win the next decade will be the ones who can hold a clear philosophy, reason about adversary behavior, and direct the tools at their disposal toward outcomes that matter. AMOSKYS is my attempt to do exactly that, in public, while building something the people who depend on it can actually trust.
